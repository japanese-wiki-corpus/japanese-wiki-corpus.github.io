Sanetomi IMADEGAWA (今出川実富)

Sanetomi IMADEGAWA (year of birth unknown - August 27, 1428) was a kugyo (court noble) who lived during the Muromachi period. He served two emperors, Emperor Gokomatsu (the 100th) and Emperor Shoko (the 101st), and advanced to Dainagon (chief councilor of state) at the rank of Shonii (Senior Second Rank). His father was Sadaijin (Minister of the left), Kinyuki IMADEGAWA. Norisue IMADEGAWA, Sadaijin, was his son.

In 1402, he was awarded the rank of Jusanmi (Junior Third Rank), becoming a kugyo. Continuing to be promoted after that, he assumed Dainagon from 1413 to 1421, after assuming Sangi (councilor), a post in Ecchu Province and Chunagon (vice-councilor of state).
