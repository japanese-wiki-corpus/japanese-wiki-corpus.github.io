Tamemoto REIZEI (冷泉為紀)

Tamemoto REIZEI (1854-1905) was a Japanese kajin (waka poet). Tamemoto was the 21st head of the Kami-Reizei family. Tamemoto's father was Tametada REIZEI. On July 7, 1884, Tamemoto was given a title of count. Tamemoto served as Daiguji (the supreme priest) of Ise-jingu Shrine from 1898 to his death. Tamemoto had a son named Tametsugi REIZEI.

Major Written Work:

"Saiten-saho Zen" (all the manners for ceremonies)
