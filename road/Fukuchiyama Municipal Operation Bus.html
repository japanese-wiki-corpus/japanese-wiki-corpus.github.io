Fukuchiyama Municipal Operation Bus (福知山市営バス)

The Fukuchiyama Municipal Operation Bus is the community oriented bus operated by Fukuchiyama City, Kyoto Prefecture.

Summary

This bus route was handed over to Fukuchiyama City from three towns of this area, when those towns were incorporated into Fukuchiyama City. All of the former town bus services had replaced the former private bus services and the former National Railway bus service, when those bus services were discontinued by those companies.

Operating routes

There are also some irregular bus routes other than the ones described in this article.

Oe Bus Routes

The origin of this bus route is the former Oe-cho Municipal Management Bus, which was handed over from the former Kyoto Transport Co., Ltd. Kameoka Management Office and the former National Railway Bus to Oe-cho (this is the town that used to belong to the former Kasa District of Kyoto Prefecture.)
Refer to the linked information web site and the bus route chart.

Oe Yamanoie Route: Sakaigawa - Gujyo Station - Tadewara - Oe Station (Kyoto Prefecture) - Futamata Station - Oe Yamaguchi Naiku Station - Juvenile Center - Oe Yamanoie

Nika South Route: Gujyo Station Front - Green Hill - Former Agricultural Cooperative Office Front - Senbara North - Tadehara - Oe Station Front - Kyoto Prefectural Oe High School - Kitaariji - Nika North - Nika South

Miwa Bus Routes

The origin of this bus route is the former Miwa-cho Municipal Management Bus, which was handed over from the West JR Bus Company and the former Kyoto Transport Co., Ltd., and the Shinki Bus Co., Ltd. to Miwa-cho (this is the town that used to belong to the former Amata District of Kyoto Prefecture.)
The public nickname of this bus is the 'Hagi-go' (Bushclover). Refer to the linked information web site and the bus route chart.

Yakuno Bus Routes

The origin of this bus route is the former Yakuno-cho Municipal Management Bus, which was handed over from the former Kyoto Transport Co., Ltd. to Yakuno-cho (this is the town that used to belong to the former Amata District of Kyoto Prefecture.)
The public nickname of this bus is the "Fureai Yakuno." Refer to the linked information web site and the bus route chart.
