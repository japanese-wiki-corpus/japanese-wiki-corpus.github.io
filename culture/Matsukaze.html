Matsukaze (松風)

"松風" (Matsukaze) literally means the wind blowing against the forest of pine trees. Within the context of Japanese traditional literature and the arts, Matsukaze was meant to describe a desolate, lonely scenery of a seashore. In the field of Waka poetry, (a traditional Japanese poetry style) Matsukaze was used as a pillow word, which means "waiting."

The following are the word "Matsukaze" used in various occasions and cases in Japan. Unless otherwise noted, the pronunciation of "松風" is "Matsukaze."
