Houn-ji Temple (Heguri-cho) (法雲寺 (平群町))

Houn-ji Temple is a Nichiren Sho sect temple located in Heguri-cho, Ikoma-gun, Nara Prefecture. Its sango (literally, "mountain name"), which is the title prefixed to the name of a Buddhist temple, is Tatsutasan.

Origin and history

September 13, 1963: Founded
The kaiki (a patron of a temple in its founding) was Nichidatsu Shonin, the 66th head priest of Nichiren Sho sect Taiseki-ji Temple.
